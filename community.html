Elders Group

The Soowahlie First Nations Elders Group is a very active and integral part of the success of the Nation. They provide guidance in every way to not only the Chief and Council, but to everyone in the community. The Elders share their knowledge and experience with anyone that is willing to listen and learn.

The Elders group is very involved with the community. They attend the community meetings, gatherings, events, and cultural practices. The Elders plan the community burnings, fruit canning, cemetery clean up, and are always very involved in carrying out these events as well. They provide great leadership and experience to the Community, and are the best role models. The Elders meet regularly in the Campsite House.

Currently, the Elders meet up at least once a month, and more if they are planning fundraising events for the annual Elders gathering. The annual Elders gathering is held in the summer in a different location throughout B.C. each year. The gathering is usually the highlight of the Elders group year because they get to travel and spend time with each other and other elders from different areas in B.C.

The Elders have a lot of knowledge. They can teach us many of our cultural practices and family history. We need to look after the Elders because they are the key to our Culture and History. They are one of the only connections we have with our history. The Elders have a vast knowledge of most of the family trees not only on Soowahlie, but also of the Sto:lo Nation area.

Community Coordinator

Soowahlie Community Coordinator consists of planning and facilitating various programs for the Soowahlie community members. The goal is always to provide programs and services to enhance the well-being of the people while practicing cultural traditions.

The following are some examples of program delivery:

  • Access funding through proposal grant writing
  • Cultural ceremonies
  • Youth, adult and elder activities
  • Community wellness events
