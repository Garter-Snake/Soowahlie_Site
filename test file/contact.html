  • Tanya Commodore

    Councillor / Lands Clerk

    Phone: 604-858-4631

    About About

    From an early age, Tanya has been an active member of Soowahlie First Nation.

    After spending part of her early years in Cheam First Nation, at age thirteen Tanya returned home to Soowahlie to live with her grandmother.

    Tanya went to post-secondary school to become a medical office assistant, but soon learnt that her true calling was to work within her community.

    In her early 20's, she began her employment with the Sweltzer Creek Campground, where she has worked in every capacity imaginable, from general labour and security, to booking staff and campground general manager.

    Tanya serves on the Soowahlie Band Council and was originally nominated by elders Harry Kelly and Kenny Wallace in 2007. Her approachable nature, honesty, tenacious spirit, and dedicated service has earned her the lasting respect and appreciation of her community and colleagues. At the present, she is honoured to be serving her sixth term on council.

    Tanya began her time in the Soowahlie Lands Department by joining the Lands Legacy Project in 2020. Since that time, she has proven herself to be a vital and valued member of the team, coming up with new and creative solutions to complex projects.

    Tanya is blessed with six children between the ages of 1 and 27, as well as four grandchildren. Her world revolves around being a wife, mother, grandmother, and serving her community. In her spare time, she loves organizing her space and listening to a variety of music, particularly “the 80's”.

    The future Tanya envisions for Soowahlie is one of hope, prosperity, and strength. She rejects progress that is motivated solely by monetary gain, but rather sees a future that is “Soowahlie driven,” honouring our past, present, and future generations. Tanya believes that if we honour the land, we honour our ancestors, and if we protect the land, we protect our children. She can't wait to see what the future holds for our people.
