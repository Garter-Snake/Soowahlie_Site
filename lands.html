Th'ewa:lí Téméxw (Soowahlie Lands)

Soowahlie First Nation is 1018 acres in size, and located south of the City of Chilliwack and north of Cultus Lake within the Fraser Valley. Currently there are approximately over 400 people living in 84 homes. Soowahlie is a semi-isolated community with a majority of our reserve lands located inside of our gates.

Land Code

Soowahlie votes yes to the Land Code in 2016

The Soowahlie Land Code is the land law of Soowahlie First Nation, and replaces land management provisions of the Indian Act. This allows for creation of Laws, regulation and policies for how lands and the resources on reserve are managed. Soowahlie's Land Code outlines the role and authority for Soowahlie Lands. The Soowahlie Lands Department, Soowahlie Lands Advisory Committee, and Soowahlie Council all have roles in decision making. Lands activities include land registrations, surveys, permits, development of laws, policies and procedures, environmental management, and enforcement.

Committee

The Soowahlie Lands Advisory Committee includes a broad representation of the main family groups, elders, and youth. As outlines in the Land Code, the committees roles and responsibilities are to be the voice of their families with lands related administration, recommendations to council decision making, etc.

Projects

Land Use Plan

Soowahlie received funding to create a Land Use Plan. This plan would designate areas of Soowahlie for different uses like commercial, residential, mixed use, etc. Through the course of 2-3 years, we did a lot of community engagement to gather the wishes of the community. Dillon Consulting has taken that information a created a beautiful draft plan that will need to go to a membership vote to approve.

Solid Waste

Soowahlie received funding for Solid Waste Management. We used a theme of “removing barriers” as inspiration for this project. This included an addition of a cardboard bin by our community garbage bins, purchased roll out bins for each home for garbage day, 4 recycling bins for each home for sorting recycling at home, and educational magnets to remind everyone about recycling.

Legacy Issues

Since entering Land Code, we found that there were a lot of unresolved issues all over Soowahlie. We successfully received funding to start dealing with some of these issues and hire another staff to assist. This project includes boundary investigations and fixing surveys.

Habitat Restoration

Soowahlie is working with LFFA (Lower Fraser Valley Fishing Alliance) on a habitat restoration plan. This project will assess current habitat areas and create a restoration plan for applying for further funding.

Wildfire Resiliency Plan

With the increase of wildfires over that past few years and growing concerns with impacts to our forested land, we've received funding to work with Frontera Firesolutions to create a wildfire resiliency plan for Soowahlie.

Environmental Clean Up

Clean up of environmentally contaminated sites from fires at the river.
