SOOWAHLIE FIRST NATION Th'éwá:lí

Intro to Administration

Soowahlie First Nation employs up to 30 people at a time. The Band Manager is the person who oversees all the duties of the staff on a day to day basis. The Band Manager also looks after the financial side of the band. The Band Office also has a Receptionist, a Land Code Coordinator, and a Maintenance Worker. The Health Office staff are listed in the Health Section of the plan. The other employees come from the Sweltzer Creek Campground, which is Soowahlie First Nation's Campsite. In order to make business run as usual, all the staff in both offices have to work well with each other and the entire community.

For the amount of work that needs to be done for Soowahlie, it feels like we don't have enough staff some of the time. The staff usually have to do multiple jobs to try to make everything run smoothly. The current staff work very well together and everyone gets along very well. They are dedicated to Soowahlie, and will usually help out the community in any way they can. They are always looking out for opportunities that would advance Soowahlie.

The Band Administrator who oversees the organization and it the Band Administration also does the Financial Management. Brenda Wallace has been in this position for several years. There is administration staff, Lands staff and the Housing staff in the Administration building. Next door there is a Health office building with health staff and the Income Assistance/Education staff member.

Education

  • Provides services for students in Elementary, Middle, Secondary and Post-Secondary school
  • Ensuring Local Education Agreement is maintained
  • Working with teachers to provide on going support, when needed
  • Process and intake of Post-Secondary applications
  • Annual post-secondary education registry report and nominal roll registry report for Indigenous Services Canada
  • Monthly Indigenous Education Advisory Committee meeting
  • Organize and plan Graduation Celebration Ceremony
  • organize funding and creating events for k-12 students
  • Engage in training sessions for updated policies and new curriculums work in a variety of fields to develop learning programs for students
  • Evaluate the existing curriculum and develop innovative solutions to improve student outcomes

The people of Soowahlie First Nation are becoming more educated as a people. From the youth to the adults, to the Chief and Council, to the Elders, the people are learning more both formally in and out of school. Education is a very important thing if you want your community to move forward in this generation.

In the elementary, middle, and secondary school education systems there is more First Nations recognition. A part of that recognition is the increase in First Nations-based curriculum. Even in the University of the Fraser Valley there is a First Nation support system that can be utilized by the university students from Soowahlie.

Soowahlie has taken control of the Education Program funding it receives from the government. This allows them to hire education worker to distribute the funding to the different levels of schooling. Having a full-time education worker is very useful because they can get to know the wants and needs of the Soowahlie parents and children. Taking control of the funding lets Soowahlie set out its own guidelines for who receives funding for University and other post-secondary programs. It also gives our community a better chance to see where the funds are being spent.

Although we are moving in the right direction, Soowahlie needs to keep working on its relationship with the schools that our youth go to. There are some stories of a disconnect between the school and the students, Soowahlie parents, Chief and Council, and the education worker can all work together to make the best possible scenario for the students.

Housing

Housing is one of the most sensitive topics to talk about in Soowahlie. Every single house built on Soowahlie has a different story to it. Many of the houses on Soowahlie need renovations. During the construction of houses, many companies took advantage of First Nations across Canada by building houses that were not up to code. The companies took many shortcuts to save themselves money while still getting paid for the work. House inspectors need to be used if we are going to be building new houses so that we don't get scammed by construction companies.

There is a limited amount of Housing available to Soowahlie members because the amount of arrears owed doesn't allow us to explore more housing and future options. Some of the arrears owed to Soowahlie are more than likely never going to be paid back. It is a very unfortunate situation for young members who are looking into getting a house or other members who want to move back home to Soowahlie. Members want to be able to live on this beautiful land near their families and it is a shame that because of other community members, they can't. One of our main goals is to be able to have community members move home.

Public Works / Maintenance

Reports To: Chief and Council and Band Manager. The Maintenance Manager is responsible for overseeing the basic repairs and maintenance of the Social Housing; Capital Housing, Community buildings and Water programs as outlined by the Community Infrastructure from Indian and Northern Affairs Canada (INAC) and Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC).

Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Experience in all aspects of maintenance, yard work, and basic construction.
  • Provide reports and project completion reports as required.
  • Know the housing programs: Social Housing, Capital Housing, Individual Housing, Renovations, New Construction, RRAP.
  • Responsible for minor repairs and have resources for major repairs to include three quotes.
  • Organize and assist with house inspection with Housing Committee representatives.
  • Liaise between Housing Administration and/or homeowner and contractor.
  • Ensure timely completion of all components of the housing and building maintenance.
  • Liaison and arrange training sessions with CMHC and INAC contacts.
  • Maintain an efficient and accurate filing system.
  • Conduct all other relevant duties as requested by the Council or Band Manager.

The Maintenance worker is responsible for providing general maintenance service for the community areas:

  • Community Buildings
  • Water systems
  • Sewer Systems
  • Roads
  • Drainage
  • Sanitation Systems (garbage)
  • Utilities
  • Machinery, Equipment, Vehcles and Tools
  • Maintenance of Soowahlie Park Areas, Playgrounds, Office Areas and Cemetery and Elder's yards (if requested)

Social Assistance

The Social Development Program helps cover daily living costs and access pre-employment supports. This program is a last resort for eligible individuals and their families.

Applicants must be:

  • Ordinarily resident on reserve
  • 19 years or older
  • Be eligible for basic or special financial assistance
  • Show that they have no other source of funding to meet basic needs

If applying for Income Assistance, applicants will be assessed according to the criteria of the province. This assessment covers:

  • Financial need
  • Employability
  • Family composition and age
  • Available financial resources in the household

To apply, contact the Band Social Development Worker to set up an appointment.

Indian and Northern Affairs Canada (INAC) develops policies and delivers social development programs to the First Nations on reserve. In an effort to address social conditions on reserve, over the years INAC has established programs. The INAC Social Development provides eligible recipients with the means to meet basic needs for food, clothing and shelter. The Income Assistance Program called Basic Needs is available to all on-reserve residents and their dependents who meet eligibility criteria, as defined by the province or territory of residence, and who are in need of basic or special financial assistance.

Soowahlie has recently started to run our own Social Development program. Our Social Development worker can closely monitor the people who are on social assistance. The Social Development worker should be someone who is not from Soowahlie so that they can do the job without the extra pressure of working with family members. Confidentiality is also very important when dealing with social assistance, personal information is kept between the client and the worker.

Soowahlie also works with Stolo Aboriginal Skills and Employment Training (SASET) to help community members. SASET can help our members write résumés so they can go out and apply for jobs. SASET also help community members develop action plans to either get them onto the right training or to the correct career path for them.
